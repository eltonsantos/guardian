Privacy Policy — Guardian

Last updated: January 18, 2026

Summary: The Guardian extension does not collect, sell, or share personal data. Everything runs locally on your device with no external connections.

Data Collected

No personal data is collected.

Data Stored on Your Device

  • Blocked domains and keywords: The list of sites and keywords you choose to block.
  • Allowlist: Domains you've whitelisted.
  • Password hash: Your password is hashed locally using PBKDF2 (never stored in plain text).
  • Recovery code hashes: SHA-256 hashes of your recovery codes (codes themselves are not stored).
  • Block logs: Optional local record of blocked attempts (URLs are obfuscated for privacy).
  • Installation date: Timestamp of when the extension was installed.

All data is stored only in your browser via chrome.storage.local and is never sent to any external server.

External Connections

None. Guardian works 100% offline and makes no network requests. All processing happens locally on your device.

Data Sharing

The extension does not share any data with third parties. There are no analytics, no tracking, and no external services.

Permissions

  • storage: To save your settings, blocked lists, and password hash locally.
  • declarativeNetRequest: To block navigation to specified domains and URLs.
  • tabs: To detect and block navigation attempts.

Advertising, Analytics, and Tracking

The extension does not display ads, does not use analytics, and does not track your browsing activity.

Data Removal

To remove all stored data, simply uninstall the extension. This will delete all local storage including your settings, password, and logs.
